1.  **The Color Grid:** Look for four colored blocks (Red, Blue, Green, Yellow) scattered around the room. The order in which they appear on a nearby monitor dictates the numerical code (e.g., Red=1, Blue=2).
2.  **The Clock Puzzle:** In the Executive Suites, the time displayed on broken clocks often corresponds to the keypad code for the nearby safe.
3.  **Audio Logs:** Listen closely to the background noise in VHS tapes. Occasionally, a robotic voice will read out numbers disguised as "error codes."

## Technical Guide: How to Enter Codes Successfully

It may seem straightforward, but Chapter 6 introduces "Degraded Keypads." These are damaged interfaces where certain buttons might not register, or the screen might flicker. Dealing with these requires a specific approach to ensure you don't accidentally lock yourself out.

*   **Input Lag:** There is a slight delay between pressing a button and the number appearing on the screen. Wait for the beep before pressing the next digit.
*   **Double Pressing:** If a button is stuck, you may need to press it twice rapidly to get it to register once.
*   **Resetting the Keypad:** If you make a mistake, look for a small red "C" or "Reset" button. If one isn't present, you must wait 10 seconds for the interface to clear automatically.
