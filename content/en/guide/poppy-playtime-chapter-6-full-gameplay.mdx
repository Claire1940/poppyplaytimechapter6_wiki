The early portion of Chapter 6 focuses on a high-stakes encounter where Kissy Missy is incapacitated, leaving you to coordinate with allies like Poppy and the mysterious "Butcher." The primary objective here is to neutralize the Prototype using a combination of water and high-voltage electricity. This section requires precise movement, as the Prototype is now half-metal and susceptible to electrical conductivity.

To progress, you must navigate the upper scaffolding of the Stage Rig. This area is characterized by verticality and crumbling infrastructure. Follow these steps to activate the trap:

| Step | Objective | Key Hazard |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Reach the Second Floor Emergency Lever | Falling debris and high-altitude walkways. |

## Tracking the Cookie Crumb Trail

After the initial confrontation at the Stage Rig, the Prototype will flee, shedding pieces of a "shield" in the form of cookie crumbs. This trail is your primary means of navigation through the residential sector of the factory. It is important to note that the Prototype is intentionally leading you toward a specific location—the house of Lily.

The hunt requires a keen eye for detail. The crumbs are often hidden behind environmental objects or near side entrances. If you lose the trail, check the corners of the room or look for structural damage that indicates the Prototype's massive frame has passed through. 

### Key Locations in the Residential Sector

*   **The Main Stage Entrance:** Locked from the inside, requiring a detour through the side maintenance tunnels.
*   **The Utility Corridor:** Contains the "Prototype Arm" collectible, which is hinted to be a part of Huggy Wuggy that was previously ripped off.
*   **Lily’s Side Entrance:** A narrow crawlspace that avoids the main trapped hallway.

## The Red Smoke and Doctor’s Experiments

As you move deeper than the standard factory floor, you encounter the true purpose of the Red Smoke. According to recovered logs from a high-ranking Doctor, the smoke was not just a sedative but a cloaking mechanism used to hide children within the facility. This section of the **poppy playtime chapter 6 full gameplay** involves navigating rooms filled with mirrors, where the experiments are kept under constant surveillance.

| Experiment Phase | Description | Player Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1: Observation** | Movement behind mirrors is visible but unreachable. | Keep moving; do not stop to stare. |
| **Phase 2: Gas Saturation** | Red Smoke fills the room, reducing visibility. | Equipping the Gas Mask is mandatory. |
| **Phase 3: Thermal Overload** | The "Heat" mechanic activates, causing environmental damage. | Locate the cooling vents to reset the room temperature. |

The "Heat" mechanic is a recurring threat in the final act. You will hear rhythmic chanting or mechanical pulses indicating that the fabric of the reality within the factory is being manipulated. To survive these sequences, you must identify the source of the thermal energy and disable it before the "Super Heat" phase begins, which results in an instant game over.

## Boss Strategy: The Prototype's Final Form

The final boss of Chapter 6 is a multi-phase encounter that tests everything you have learned about the GrabPack and environmental hazards. Unlike previous chapters, the Prototype does not simply chase you; he uses the factory itself as a weapon.

1.  **Phase One (The Flood):** Use the GrabPack to swing between rafters while the floor is electrified. You must wait for the Prototype to grab a metal pole before activating the current.
2.  **Phase Two (The Mirror Maze):** The Prototype will hide behind one-way mirrors. You must look for the reflection of his glowing eyes to determine which panel to smash using the GrabPack's heavy hand.
3.  **Phase Three (Thermal Core):** The room will begin to overheat. You must redirect the red smoke into the cooling vents to cause a pressure explosion.

> **💡 Tip:** During the Thermal Core phase, listen for the Doctor's voice. He often provides audio cues about which vent is currently operational, allowing you to bypass the trial-and-error process.
