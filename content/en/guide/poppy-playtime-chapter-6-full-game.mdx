## Navigating the Stage and Utility Areas

The core gameplay of the **poppy playtime chapter 6 full game** takes place in the "Grand Stage" and the surrounding utility rooms. This area is designed to be a vertical labyrinth, forcing players to confront their fear of heights. You will spend a significant amount of time climbing ladders and balancing on narrow stage rigs to restore power to the building's infrastructure.

To progress through the mid-game, you must locate the "Cold Storage" area. Specifically, **Locker C9** holds a vital case that contains the components needed to repair the GrabPack’s final upgrade. Without this, the high-voltage puzzles in the final act are impossible to complete.

> **💡 Tip:** When navigating the high ladders in the Stage Rigging area, keep your movement steady. Sudden shifts can cause the player to slip, leading to an instant "Game Over" from fall damage.

## The "Fried Prototype" Strategy

The final encounter in the **poppy playtime chapter 6 full game** isn't a traditional fight. Because the Prototype is "half metal," he is highly susceptible to electricity. However, a simple spark won't work; you need a massive surge. The plan involves three distinct phases that must be executed in perfect sequence.

### Step-by-Step Electrocution Guide

1.  **Flood the Stage:** Locate the utility room to turn on the main water shut-off. If the pipes are dry, you must head to the second floor and pull the **Emergency Lever**.
2.  **Rig the Cable:** While the Stage is soaking, use your GrabPack to pull the high-voltage cable from the upper stage rig. 
3.  **The Bait:** Huggy Wuggy and Butcher will distract the Prototype, leading him onto the wet surface.
4.  **The Execution:** Drop the live cable into the floodwater to "cook" the Prototype's metallic components.

| **Phase 2: Voltage** | Stage Rigging | Requires the GrabPack's insulated wire upgrade. |
| **Phase 3: Trapping** | The Grand Stage | Prototype must be standing in the center of the flood. |

## Tracking the Prototype: The Crumb Trail

The Prototype is a "sick, clever thing," as described by the characters in the game. After his initial confrontation, he flees, leaving behind a trail of cookie crumbs. These aren't accidental; he is leading the player toward a specific side entrance of his "house." 

During this sequence, players should look for the following items to ensure they are on the right path:
*   **The Shield Fragments:** Pieces of the Prototype's defensive plating that he sheds while running.
*   **Prototype's Arm:** A severed limb (previously ripped off by Huggy) that can be found near the Stage exit. This item is essential for a secret ending.
*   **Lily's Ribbon:** Found near the entrance to the "Tea Party" room, signaling you are close to the final boss arena.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not touch anything in the Prototype's "house" until Huggy or Butcher gives the signal. The area is heavily booby-trapped with proximity sensors.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Huggy Wuggy really a hero in the poppy playtime chapter 6 full game?**

A: While "hero" might be a strong word, Huggy acts as a primary ally. His motivation is purely to save his sister, Lily, which aligns his goals with the player's objective to stop the Prototype.

**Q: How do I find the emergency lever on the second floor?**

A: From the Grand Stage, look for the yellow ladder on the far left. Climb it to reach the maintenance catwalks. Follow the red pipes until you see a room labeled "Utility 02." The lever is located behind a breakable glass panel.

**Q: What happens if I don't pick up the Prototype's arm?**

A: Picking up the arm is optional for the main story, but it is required if you want to reattach it to Kissy Missy later in the game to unlock a special cutscene where she assists you in the final escape.

**Q: Is this the final chapter of Poppy Playtime?**

A: Chapter 6 is marketed as the conclusion of the "Prototype Saga." While there may be spin-offs or DLC in the future, the main storyline regarding the 1995 incident and the Prototype's reign is resolved here.
