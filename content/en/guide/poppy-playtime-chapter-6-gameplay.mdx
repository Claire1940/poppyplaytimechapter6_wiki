## The Tactical Showdown: How to Defeat the Prototype

One of the most complex segments of the **poppy playtime chapter 6 gameplay** is the "Stage Rig" encounter. The Prototype’s metallic components make him susceptible to electrical damage, but a simple spark won't suffice. You must utilize the factory's internal plumbing and electrical systems in tandem.

Follow these steps to successfully navigate the encounter:
1.  **Locate the Utility Room:** The fire suppression system is initially disabled. You must find the shut-off valve to flood the stage area.
2.  **Restore Power:** The emergency lever is located on the second floor. Be prepared for vertical platforming, as this area features high ladders and narrow walkways that test the player's movement mechanics.
3.  **Deploy the Live Cable:** Once the stage is soaked, you must drop the high-voltage cable from the rig into the water while the Prototype is standing within the flood zone.

## The "Cookie Crumb" Trail: Exploration Mechanics

A unique feature of the **poppy playtime chapter 6 gameplay** is the tracking system. After the initial encounter, the Prototype flees, but he leaves behind fragments of a shield—referred to by characters as "cookie crumbs." These glowing physical markers serve as your primary guide through the labyrinthine corridors leading to Lily’s house.

This mechanic replaces the traditional objective markers, forcing players to pay closer attention to the floor and environment. If you lose the trail, check the corners of doorways and side entrances. The Prototype is large, and his frame often scrapes against the walls, leaving behind these visual cues.

> **💡 Tip:** Use your GrabPack to interact with the environment while following the trail. Often, the Prototype will move through vents or doors that require the player to find a secondary entrance nearby.

## Environmental Hazards and Platforming

Chapter 6 introduces a higher degree of verticality. Players who struggle with heights or precision platforming may find the utility room and stage rig sections challenging. The "Heart Rate" mechanic returns, where the player's vision blurs and the character's breathing becomes heavy during high-stress climbs.

To manage this, players should:
*   **Move Steadily:** Do not rush on ladders or narrow beams; slipping results in an instant "Game Over."
*   **Listen for Audio Cues:** The Prototype's growls and the sound of shifting metal indicate when a platform is about to become unstable.
*   **Check for Side Entrances:** If a main door is locked or seems too small for the Prototype to have entered, look for a side entrance that can be opened from the inside.
